# Project Proposal: Theobase Global Church Utility Platform

**The Goal:** A modern, all-in-one digital assistant built specifically for
local Seventh-day Adventist congregations worldwide.

**The Cost:** A flat, budget-friendly **$5 USD/month** per church (Billed
Annually at $60 USD).

**The Philosophy:** Keep it incredibly simple, respect the volunteers' time,
work perfectly even with poor internet, and keep church data absolutely secure.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Most church software is built for high-level corporate offices, focusing on
regional audits and executive reports. This leaves local church
volunteers—clerks, treasurers, deacons, and elders—drowning in messy group
chats, lost paper trails, complex spreadsheets, and manual weekend calculations.

**Theobase** is built from the ground up for the local church. It automates
weekly platform scheduling, simplifies local treasury work, tracks membership
records and baptisms, manages youth ministries, organizes board governance,
secures nominating committee ballots, coordinates pastoral districts, prepares
conference statistical reports, and keeps every department — from Pathfinders
to Dorcas to Health Ministries — running on one unified platform.

Crucially, **Theobase does not act as a bank or a payment system.** Instead, it
serves as a secure **Digital Filing Cabinet**. Members donate using the local
methods they already trust (like mobile money apps, M-PAiSA, or direct bank
transfers) and simply upload a photo of their receipt. Treasurers verify the
photo against their official bank statements with one click. This eliminates
financial liabilities, costs nothing in extra processing fees, and ensures 100%
of sacred funds go directly to the church.

---

## 2. Core Pillars of the Platform

To make this platform a global standard, it is built on three strict
non-technical rules:

- **Works Anywhere (Even Offline):** Designed for rural or remote churches. If
  the internet drops out during a Sabbath service, the app saves everything
  locally on the user's phone or tablet and automatically syncs the moment a
  signal returns.
- **Total Safety and Privacy:** Built to comply with GDPR and equivalent
  international data-protection regulations (Kenya's DPA, Nigeria's NDPR,
  Brazil's LGPD, and others). A church's data stays securely encrypted and
  physically hosted within its own geographic region, keeping member records
  fully protected.
- **Zero Financial Middlemen:** Because money moves directly from the member's
  personal banking app to the church's official bank account, no tech company
  takes a percentage of the tithe or offerings.

## 4. How the "Digital Tithe Envelope" Works

Because Seventh-day Adventist giving requires precise fund allocation across
tithe, local church budget, and department offerings, the app replaces physical
paper envelopes with a simple **Digital Tithe Envelope**.

1. **The Member’s Experience:** A member sends money to the church’s bank using
   their phone, takes a screenshot of the receipt, and uploads it to the app.
    Below the photo, they type out their desired split: _Tithe: $70, Church
    Budget: $20, Pathfinders: $10_. The app makes sure the breakdown
    matches the receipt total before submission.
2. **The Treasurer’s Experience:** Each week, the treasurer opens a clean
   digital list. They see the member's name, the uploaded receipt image, and the
   requested fund breakdown. They verify the deposit on their bank statement and
   click **Approve**. The software instantly and accurately updates the separate
   department balances.

## 5. Platform Architecture & Governance

Beyond the feature set, the platform is governed by architectural decisions
that ensure it works in practice, not just on paper.

### Roles, Permissions & Confidentiality

Every church officer operates within a defined scope. Theobase enforces a
granular permission model out of the box:

- **Members** can view their personal profile, giving history, and ministry
  involvement — but never another member's confidential data.
- **Department Leaders** (Pathfinder directors, Sabbath School superintendents,
  Dorcas coordinators) manage only their own department's records.
- **Clerks & Treasurers** access membership and finance records respectively,
  with an audit log recording every sensitive action.
- **Pastors & Elders** receive a read-only oversight dashboard across all
  departments without the ability to alter financial entries.
- **District Pastors** see aggregated, anonymized cross-church dashboards
  without accessing individual member records in congregations outside their
  immediate pastoral charge.

The **Secure Nominating Vault** and **Community Welfare CRM** operate under
additional encryption and access isolation, ensuring that only explicitly
authorized officers can view nomination ballots or welfare case histories.

### Authentication & Account Security

Users log in with **email-based passwordless authentication**. A secure
magic link or one-time code is sent to the user's email address — no
passwords to remember, no password database to breach. This approach is
deliberately aligned with the elderly-volunteer accessibility goal: a
church elder who struggles with passwords simply opens their email and taps
the link. Accounts are protected by rate-limited login attempts, automatic
session expiry after inactivity, and mandatory two-factor verification
for roles handling financial or confidential data (treasurers, clerks,
nominating committee members).

### Multi-Language & Global Readiness

As a platform targeting Adventist congregations across Africa, the Pacific,
South America, Asia, and beyond, Theobase is built with internationalization
as a first-class concern:

- **Interface translation** into major world-field languages: French,
  Spanish, Portuguese, Swahili, Tok Pisin, Bislama, Hindi, Tagalog, and more.
- **Right-to-left support** for Arabic and other RTL scripts.
- **Date and currency formatting** adapting to local conventions (e.g.
  Fijian dollars, Papua New Guinean kina, Solomon Islands dollars).
- **Sabbath sunset time data** sourced from a global geolocation database,
  ensuring the Sabbath-Calibrated Timing Engine works accurately in every
  time zone.

### Church-Size Adaptability

A village church of 25 members in the Solomon Islands has fundamentally
different needs than a 600-member urban congregation in Nairobi. Theobase
adapts through:

- **Progressive feature unlocking:** Small churches use only core modules
  (scheduling, giving, minutes) without being overwhelmed by district-level
  tools.
- **Scalable data models:** Whether tracking 3 Pathfinders or 150, the
  underlying architecture scales without performance degradation.
- **Simplified vs. advanced views:** Department leaders see a streamlined
  interface; administrators toggle to detailed audit views when needed.

### Accessibility & Elderly Volunteer Inclusion

Many church volunteers are elderly members with limited tech experience,
reduced vision, or dexterity challenges. Theobase prioritizes:

- **Large-touch targets and high-contrast modes** for users with vision
  impairment.
- **Voice-dictation support** for entering meeting minutes and pastoral notes.
- **A simplified "essentials-only" mode** that hides advanced features,
  presenting a large-button interface for volunteers who only need to
  perform one or two weekly tasks.
- **Printable reports with QR codes** so volunteers comfortable with paper
  can generate a printed sheet and scan the QR code to confirm a digital
  action (e.g. approving a receipt after reviewing a printed bank statement).

### Mobile & Device Strategy

Theobase is delivered as a **Progressive Web Application (PWA)** — no app
store download required. A volunteer simply visits the church's Theobase URL
and adds it to their home screen. The PWA approach provides:

- **Instant deployment** across iOS, Android, and desktop browsers from a
  single codebase.
- **Full offline capability** through Service Worker caching and local
  IndexedDB storage, automatically synchronizing when connectivity returns.
- **Minimal storage footprint** — critical for users on entry-level
  smartphones with limited internal memory common in developing regions.
- **Over-the-air updates** that don't require re-downloading from an app
  store over expensive or slow mobile data connections.

### Real-Time & Notification Strategy

All live communication within the platform runs over **WebSockets**. When a
user has the app open, changes stream instantly: duty rota swaps appear as
they happen, board meeting minutes sync across participants, and pulpit
order-of-service changes reach the AV booth before the elder finishes
speaking. The same WebSocket channel delivers in-app notification toasts
for time-sensitive actions.

Every notification is also delivered by **email** as a guaranteed fallback.
If a volunteer isn't looking at their phone when a duty reminder fires, the
email ensures they still receive it. This dual-channel approach means the
platform avoids the iOS PWA push-notification gap entirely — the WebSocket
handles the active-user case, and email guarantees delivery for everyone
else.

### Data Resilience & Backup

- **Automated daily encrypted backups** stored in-region, with point-in-time
  recovery for at least 90 days.
- **Offline-first sync guarantees:** data entered on a user's device during
  a connectivity outage is never lost — it queues locally and reconciles with
  the server using conflict-free replicated data types (CRDTs).
- **Disaster recovery failover** to a secondary data center within the same
  geographic region, activated automatically within minutes of a primary
  outage.

### Data Portability & Exit

A church always owns its data. At any time, authorized officers can export:

- The complete membership roll (CSV).
- Full financial transaction history with receipt images (portable ZIP archive).
- All board minutes and governance records (PDF or DOCX).
- Baptismal and profession-of-faith records (CSV).

Exports are generated on-demand and encrypted during download. If a church
chooses to stop using Theobase, their data is permanently purged from the
platform within 30 days of a confirmed deletion request, after the export
has been verified.

## 6. Onboarding & Migration

Switching from paper, spreadsheets, and group chats to a unified digital
platform is the hardest part of adoption. Theobase includes a structured
onboarding pathway.

### Pilot Program (Pre-Launch)

Three to five volunteer churches across different world divisions —
representing rural, urban, small, and large congregations — will trial the
platform for two months before public release. Their feedback directly shapes
the final interface and migration tooling.

### Guided Church Setup Wizard

When a church first signs up, a step-by-step wizard walks the clerk through:

1. Entering basic church details (name, address, conference, time zone).
2. Importing the member roll from a simple CSV template (downloaded directly
   from the wizard).
3. Setting up department rosters (Pathfinders, Sabbath School divisions,
   Dorcas, Health).
4. Configuring the church's bank account details for receipt verification.
5. Inviting officers via secure email links.

### Migration Templates & Bulk Import

For churches migrating from existing spreadsheets or legacy church software:

- **Pre-formatted CSV templates** for membership rolls, historical giving
  records, Pathfinder class progress, and Sabbath School attendance.
- **Bulk photo upload** for digitizing historical board minutes and audit
  documents.
- **A dedicated migration support channel** staffed during the first 90 days
  of a church's subscription to assist with data clean-up and import
  validation.

### Ongoing Training & Support

- A searchable **knowledge base** with short video walkthroughs (1–3 minutes
  each) in multiple languages, demonstrating one task at a time.
- **Quarterly live webinars** for clerks and treasurers covering seasonal
  workflows (annual elections, audit preparation, camporee registration).
- **In-app contextual help** highlighting "first-time tips" when a user
  opens a module for the first time.

## 7. Rollout Plan

To make adoption as smooth as possible, features will be released in four
logical steps:

- **Phase 0 — Pilot & Tooling (Pre-Launch, 2 months):** Three to five pilot
  churches trial the platform. The onboarding wizard, migration CSV templates,
  bulk import tools, and the knowledge base are built and refined from real
  feedback before any public release.
- **Phase 1 — The Weekly Essentials (Months 1–3):** Fixes every-Sabbath
  headaches: Saturday scheduling, board meetings, the Digital Receipt
  Registry, offline capability, the Member Self-Service Portal, and the core
  roles & permissions framework.
- **Phase 2 — Department Growth (Months 4–6):** Deploys specialized tools
  that local leaders love: the Pathfinder Matrix, Sabbath School registers,
  the Tithe Envelope Camera Assistant, the Harvest Decision Tracker (baptism
  tracking), and the Household Relationship Mapper.
- **Phase 3 — Broad Field Coordination (Months 7–9):** Introduces advanced
  features: the Secure Nominating Vault, the Pastoral District Hub, the
  Crisis Resilience Matrix, the Conference Report Generator, the Digital
  Transfer & Reception Desk, and the Policy Compliance Guardrails.

## 8. Financial Sustainability

By charging an **Annual Subscription of $60 USD** (the equivalent of just $5 a
month), local congregations can easily fund the platform out of their basic
administrative budget. Every church receives a **30-day free trial** with no
payment details required — the church only pays once they've migrated their
data, trained their officers, and confirmed the platform works for their
specific needs.

Because the platform runs on serverless edge infrastructure, a typical church
spends most of the month incurring near-zero compute cost — the system only
bills for the brief moments it actively processes requests. This lean
architecture keeps overhead low while delivering reliable customer support
and continuous security updates directly back to local fields.
