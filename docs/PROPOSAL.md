# Project Proposal: Theobase Global Church Utility Platform

**The Goal:** A modern, all-in-one digital assistant built specifically for local Seventh-day Adventist congregations worldwide to clear away volunteer burnout.

**The Cost:** A flat, budget-friendly **$7 USD per month** per church.

**The Core Philosophy:** Keep it incredibly simple, respect the volunteers' time, work perfectly even when the internet drops out, and keep church records completely secure.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Most church software systems are built from the top down for high-level corporate offices. They focus heavily on regional audits, macro-demographics, and executive compliance reports. Unfortunately, this leaves the actual local church volunteers—the clerks, treasurers, deacons, and youth leaders—completely unassisted, drowning in messy group chats, lost paper trails, confusing financial systems, and exhausting weekend paperwork.

**Theobase** is built from the ground up to serve the local volunteer. Think of it as a helpful, organized digital assistant that automates weekly scheduling, simplifies local department budgets, tracks youth merit badges, and keeps church board meetings organized.

### Why Theobase is Safe, Legal, and Simple

Crucially, **Theobase is not a bank and does not handle or process donation money directly.** Managing digital payment networks involves immense legal hazards, security risks, tax liabilities, and high processing fees that would drain a local church's resources.

Instead, Theobase functions as an intelligent **Digital Filing Cabinet and Verification Ledger**. Members donate money using the local payment methods they already know, use, and trust—such as their personal mobile money apps (like M-PAiSA), standard shortcodes, or direct bank transfers. They simply take a screenshot of their transaction confirmation receipt and upload it to the platform.

The volunteer treasurer views the uploaded picture on their dashboard, matches it against the church's official bank statement, and clicks "Approve." This simple, brilliant method eliminates all financial liabilities for the software, keeps transaction percentage losses at zero, and ensures that 100% of sacred tithes and offerings go directly into the church's bank account.

## 2. The Core Guarantees

To ensure this platform can run smoothly in any church around the globe—from a large city temple to a remote island chapel—it follows three strict structural rules:

* **Works Perfectly Offline:** Built for areas with unstable electricity or expensive, metered mobile data. If the internet cuts out entirely during a busy Saturday morning service, the app doesn't freeze or lose data. It quietly saves everything onto the user's phone or tablet and safely synchronizes with the cloud the moment a stable signal returns.
* **Complete Privacy Safeguards:** Built to protect member data automatically. Personal contact details, family records, and child-safety background checks are kept behind highly secure digital locks, hosted within the church's respective geographic region to honor international privacy laws.
* **No Hidden Fees on Offerings:** Because the software does not touch or route the donation money itself, no intermediate technology company takes a hidden cut or percentage fee from tithes and offerings. Every cent lands safely in the church's bank.

## 3. The 19 Problems, Everyday Examples, and Solutions

Here is an in-depth breakdown of the 19 practical operational gaps local churches face every week, real-world examples of the headaches they cause, and exactly how Theobase solves them.

### Administration & Governance

#### 1. The Church Board Paper Trail

* **The Everyday Problem:** Church board decisions are often scattered across months of unorganized documents, making historical transparency impossible.
* **Real-World Example:** Elder Sione needs to verify whether the church board voted to approve a new PA system budget eight months ago. He has to spend hours searching through old email threads, loose paper folders, and random computer files.
* **The Theobase Solution:** **Boardroom Management Ledger** — An automated tool where the pastor can click to build a meeting agenda, record digital votes on policy changes, and maintain a completely searchable, unalterable digital archive of all past board minutes.

#### 2. Nominating Committee Privacy

* **The Everyday Problem:** The annual process of electing new church officers requires absolute confidentiality to protect people's feelings and maintain institutional privacy.
* **Real-World Example:** During election season, committee members pass around small paper slips to vote on a new head elder. If a tally sheet is accidentally left on a bench or seen by the wrong person, it can cause deep offense and gossip.
* **The Theobase Solution:** **Secure Nominating Vault** — A confidential digital ballot box that allows committee members to vote anonymously on their phones and tracks whether a candidate has accepted or declined a role in real-time.

#### 3. Saturday Platform Scheduling

* **The Everyday Problem:** Coordinating the dozens of volunteers needed to run a weekend worship service is an exhausting game of phone tag.
* **Real-World Example:** The volunteer scheduled to read the morning scripture text wakes up sick at 7:00 AM on Saturday. The head elder must spend his entire morning frantically calling different church members trying to find an emergency backup before the opening hymn begins.
* **The Theobase Solution:** **Smart-Swap Duty Rota** — An intelligent calendar that automatically texts volunteers a reminder of their upcoming duties early in the week. If a volunteer clicks "Decline," the system instantly alerts the organizer and offers the slot to pre-qualified, ordained backups.

#### 4. Volunteer Safety Clearances

* **The Everyday Problem:** Keeping track of background checks and safety certifications for youth leaders is legally vital but easily overlooked.
* **Real-World Example:** A well-meaning church member offers to drive the junior youth to a weekend retreat. Nobody realizes their child-safety background check expired six months ago because the records are buried in an unmonitored spreadsheet.
* **The Theobase Solution:** **Volunteer Safety Shield** — A scheduling guardrail that automatically cross-checks volunteer names against local background clearance dates, gently blocking uncertified helpers from being assigned to children's ministries until their paperwork is updated.

### Finance & Treasury

#### 5. Local Budget Tracking

* **The Everyday Problem:** Corporate church accounting software is designed for professional corporate accountants, making it far too complicated for a volunteer local treasurer.
* **Real-World Example:** The youth leader wants to know how much money is left in their specific department budget for a social night. Because the corporate accounting program is too complex to navigate, they have to guess, or wait weeks for the treasurer to manually calculate the balance.
* **The Theobase Solution:** **Volunteer Treasury Interface** — A highly simplified, visual dashboard built specifically for everyday volunteers that tracks exactly how much money is coming in, how much is going out, and what is left in each department's local budget.

#### 6. Saturday Tithe Counting

* **The Everyday Problem:** Manually counting and reconciling hundreds of physical paper tithe envelopes on Saturday evenings takes hours of exhausting work.
* **Real-World Example:** Two treasurers sit in a locked room for three hours after the service, opening crumpled paper envelopes, deciphering messy handwriting, and typing columns of numbers into a calculator.
* **The Theobase Solution:** **Tithe Envelope Camera Assistant** — A smart mobile tool that allows the treasurer to snap a quick photo of a handwritten paper envelope. The app instantly reads the handwriting, digitizes the dollar amounts, and assigns them to the correct member profile.

#### 7. Missing Audit Receipts

* **The Everyday Problem:** Local churches frequently fail internal audits not because they misplaced money, but because they misplaced the paper proof.
* **Real-World Example:** A regional auditor arrives, and the treasurer spends days frantically hunting for a physical paper receipt for a lawnmower repair from ten months ago that was left in a deacon’s truck.
* **The Theobase Solution:** **Audit Binder Cross-Referencer** — A digital storage vault that explicitly links every single expense logged in the system directly to a photo of its receipt and the exact board meeting minute that authorized the purchase.

#### 8. Tracking Digital Deposits

* **The Everyday Problem:** Members increasingly donate using direct bank deposits or mobile money apps, causing a chaotic flood of unorganized notifications for the treasurer.
* **Real-World Example:** On Friday night, five members send their offerings via a mobile transfer app and text screenshots of the confirmations to the treasurer's personal phone. The images get completely buried in chat histories and forgotten during the weekend rush.
* **The Theobase Solution:** **Digital Receipt Verification Registry** — A simple, dedicated upload window where members upload photos of their direct transfers, placing them into an orderly, neat queue for the treasurer to approve on Sunday morning.

### Ministries & Auxiliaries

#### 9. Pathfinder & Adventurer Tracking

* **The Everyday Problem:** The Pathfinder youth club operates like a massive scouting organization with badges and ranks, but tracking student progress on paper is highly unreliable.
* **Real-World Example:** A club director tracks 40 children working on their "First Aid" and "Camping Skills" honors using physical cardboard charts. The chart gets completely ruined when a water bottle leaks inside her backpack during a rainy campout.
* **The Theobase Solution:** **Pathfinder/Adventurer Matrix** — A specialized youth portal that safely tracks progressive classes (from Friend to Guide), merit badges earned, uniform sizes, and digital campout permission slips.

#### 10. Sabbath School Class Registers

* **The Everyday Problem:** Regional church records only track total attendance numbers, leaving local teachers unable to monitor personal student care and follow-up.
* **Real-World Example:** A primary class teacher is absent due to illness. The substitute teacher walks into the room blindly with no idea which children attended last week, who has been missing for a month, or what lesson study they are currently on.
* **The Theobase Solution:** **Sabbath School Division Dashboard** — A clean digital attendance register divided by age groups (from Toddlers to Adults) that allows teachers to mark attendance in seconds and instantly view student study guides.

#### 11. Community Welfare & Dorcas Privacy

* **The Everyday Problem:** Local church welfare societies handle highly sensitive, confidential information regarding food and financial help without proper data security.
* **Real-World Example:** The church welfare team provides a box of groceries and utility assistance to a family going through a painful divorce. The details are scribbled down in a standard paper notebook left on a shelf in a community closet.
* **The Theobase Solution:** **Community Welfare CRM** — A completely private, secure case-management portal that logs pantry stock, tracks assistance histories, and keeps vulnerable families' names safe from public view.

#### 12. Evangelism & Bible Study Pipeline

* **The Everyday Problem:** Contact information gathered during community health expos or prophecy seminars is frequently lost before any real spiritual follow-up happens.
* **Real-World Example:** After a successful community health screening, 50 guests fill out paper sign-in sheets requesting personalized Bible studies. Two months later, the paper clipboards are misplaced in a drawer, and the community's interest goes cold.
* **The Theobase Solution:** **Evangelism Connection Pipeline** — A digital tracking system designed specifically for missionary workers to record a community member's journey (e.g., *Attended Expo -> Requested Reading Material -> Bible Studies Commenced -> Joining Baptismal Class*), ensuring no one is ever forgotten.

### Sabbath Operations

#### 13. "Sunday-First" Software Clashes

* **The Everyday Problem:** Standard, generic church apps do not understand or respect the Friday-sunset-to-Saturday-sunset holy hours, causing disruptive alerts.
* **Real-World Example:** A generic church management app automatically fires off loud, disruptive text reminders about business meetings or choir practice times at 7:30 PM on a Friday night, right when a family is trying to enjoy quiet opening-of-Sabbath worship.
* **The Theobase Solution:** **Sabbath-Calibrated Timing Engine** — An intelligent notification manager that automatically calculates local sunset times based on the church's location, ensuring all administrative texts stay completely silent during holy hours.

#### 14. Communion Service Logistics

* **The Everyday Problem:** Coordinating the unique venue splits, physical room setups, and volunteer teams required for a quarterly communion service is incredibly complex.
* **Real-World Example:** On communion morning, the deaconesses scramble because nobody can remember how many clean linens are left in the storage vestry or which team was assigned to bake the unleavened bread.
* **The Theobase Solution:** **Communion Service Planner** — A specific logistics dashboard that coordinates room setups for the foot-washing service, coordinates volunteer assignments, and tracks communion element inventories.

#### 15. Pulpit-to-AV Booth Communication

* **The Everyday Problem:** Last-minute changes made by the speaker on the pulpit rarely reach the media and audio-visual team in the back booth in time.
* **Real-World Example:** A guest preacher decides to change their final closing hymn right before walking up to the pulpit. The media team plays the old song lyrics on the screen, causing visible confusion throughout the congregation.
* **The Theobase Solution:** **Pulpit-to-AV Live-Sync** — An instant wireless connection that allows a pastor to adjust a scripture reference or hymn on a pulpit tablet, immediately updating the screens in the media booth without anyone running notes down the aisle.

### District Strategy

#### 16. Multi-Church Pastoral Districts

* **The Everyday Problem:** Pastors who oversee anywhere from 3 to 8 separate congregations simultaneously find it nearly impossible to organize their travel and preaching schedules.
* **Real-World Example:** A district pastor drives 40 miles to a rural church site on a rainy morning, only to walk in and realize a local lay-preacher was already scheduled to speak there that day.
* **The Theobase Solution:** **Pastoral District Hub** — A master dashboard that coordinates preaching calendars across multiple locations, tracks pastoral home visits, and organizes travel mileage logs in one single place.

#### 17. Church Hall Bookings & Regulations

* **The Everyday Problem:** Renting out or booking church property must follow strict rules regarding safety, insurance, and local church policy.
* **Real-World Example:** A church member books the community hall for a Saturday night business garage sale, completely unaware that local church guidelines strictly forbid commercial business operations on church grounds.
* **The Theobase Solution:** **Policy-Aware Facility Coordinator** — A friendly calendar booking engine that runs a quick, simple policy safety check before a building request is automatically routed to the board for final approval.

#### 18. Emergency Disasters & ADRA Assets

* **The Everyday Problem:** Regional leadership offices have no instant way to see which local churches have critical supplies during a major weather crisis.
* **Real-World Example:** A severe tropical cyclone strikes the coast. Regional disaster coordinators spend days calling around trying to figure out which local church has an active backup generator or an independent water tank to use as a community shelter.
* **The Theobase Solution:** **Crisis Resilience Matrix** — A secure emergency dashboard showing exactly what utility assets (water tanks, solar systems, generators) exist at each local church, allowing rapid relief coordination during a crisis.

### Core Infrastructure

#### 19. Connectivity & Bandwidth Barriers

* **The Everyday Problem:** Standard modern websites assume users have lightning-fast, cheap, unmetered internet, which causes platforms to crash in rural areas.
* **Real-World Example:** A local church clerk tries to update the membership roster during a thunderstorm, but the web browser continuously times out due to slow data speeds, wiping out an hour of careful typing.
* **The Theobase Solution:** **Offline-First Data Architecture** — A lightweight app design that lets volunteers do 100% of their work, typing, and data logging while entirely offline. The app saves the data safely on the device and syncs it automatically using tiny, compressed data packets when a cheap, stable connection is found.

## 4. The "Virtual Envelope" Walkthrough

To see how simple this system is for everyday church members and volunteer treasurers, let’s look at a step-by-step example of how a donation travels through the app without any complicated banking interfaces.

```
[ Brother David ] Transfer funds natively via bank app ──> Upload Screenshot to Theobase
                                                                 │
                                                                 ▼
[ Volunteer Treasurer ] Open Dashboard ──> Cross-Check Bank Statement ──> Click [Approve]
                                                                 │
                                                                 ▼
                                         Ledger Automatically Splits: Tithe
                                                                      Local Church
                                                                      Pathfinders

```

* **Step 1: The Member Transfers the Money:** Brother David wants to give his tithe and offering. He opens his personal, trusted mobile money wallet or banking app on his phone and transfers his funds directly into his local church's official business bank account. He snaps a quick screenshot of the payment confirmation message on his screen.
* **Step 2: The Member Fills Out the Digital Envelope:** David opens the Theobase app on his phone, types in the total amount given, and uploads the screenshot of his receipt. Below the picture, the app displays a digital breakdown sheet that looks exactly like the physical paper envelopes he has used for decades. He allocates his transfer:
* **Tithe (Conference Trust Fund)**
* **Local Church Budget**
* **Pathfinders Youth Club** The app quickly confirms that his breakdown numbers match his total receipt amount, and he taps **Submit**.


* **Step 3: The Treasurer Verifies the Entry:** On Sunday morning, the volunteer treasurer opens their Theobase dashboard. They see David's name, the uploaded receipt image, and his requested breakdown. The treasurer opens their official bank statement or checks the church's ledger phone. They see the matching deposit from David and tap **Approve**. The system instantly updates the separate department ledgers, keeping the books transparent, audit-ready, and error-free.

## 5. Subscription & Pricing Figures

To keep the platform accessible and prevent long, complicated budget debates on the church board, Theobase utilizes a clear, flexible micro-subscription plan.

* **The Rate:** A flat **$7 USD per month** per church.
* **Flexible Billing Options:** Churches can choose how they want to manage this minor office expense:
* **Month-to-Month:** Paid as a recurring **$7 USD monthly payment**. This is perfect for local church leaders who want to use their local department debit cards to get started immediately without waiting for an annual budget layout.
* **Annually Prepaid:** Paid as a single, one-time payment of **$84 USD for the full year**. This is ideal for traditional church boards that prefer to approve a single annual line-item expense and clear the paperwork out of the way.


* **What is Included:** Every subscription includes **unlimited volunteer accounts** (pastors, clerks, treasurers, elders, deacons, and youth directors), **unlimited member profiles**, and access to all 19 operational modules with no tier restrictions or hidden upgrades.

### Payment Processing

Payments are processed via **Stripe** for card payments (Visa, Mastercard, Amex) and **Stripe Invoicing** for annual prepaid church invoices. For regions where card infrastructure is thin (Pacific Islands, parts of Sub-Saharan Africa), **mobile money integration** (M-PAiSA, M-Pesa, MTN Mobile Money) will be added in Phase 2 via local payment aggregators.

**Failed payment handling** follows a standard dunning sequence:
1. First failure → Retry after 3 days + email notification to church admin
2. Second failure → Retry after 5 days + SMS notification
3. Third failure → Retry after 7 days + final notice
4. All retries exhausted → Subscription transitions to Read-Only View; data preserved; church can reactivate at any time

**Tax handling**: Where applicable, VAT/GST is added to the $7 USD base price per local jurisdiction. Annual invoices are issued automatically via Stripe Invoicing with tax line items. For churches requiring tax exemption (many SDA churches qualify as religious non-profits), a tax ID field is provided during signup.

## 9. Technology Platform

Theobase is a **Progressive Web Application (PWA)** — a single codebase that runs on phones, tablets, and desktops with full offline capability. A comprehensive technical architecture document is maintained at **[docs/TECHNICAL_ARCHITECTURE.md](TECHNICAL_ARCHITECTURE.md)**.

### 9.1 Tech Stack Summary

| Layer | Choice | Key Property |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| Frontend | React 18 + TypeScript + Vite | PWA with service worker offline caching |
| UI | Tailwind CSS + Radix UI | Small bundle, accessible, responsive |
| Backend | Cloudflare Workers + Hono | Global edge deployment, pay-per-request |
| Database | Cloudflare D1 (distributed SQLite) | Per-church isolation, regional data residency |
| File Storage | Cloudflare R2 | Zero egress fees for photo uploads |
| Real-Time | Cloudflare Durable Objects (WebSocket) | Pulpit-to-AV sync, live notifications |
| Offline Sync | Automerge (CRDT) | Conflict-free merges without server |
| OCR | Tesseract.js v5 (client-side) | Works offline, supports 100+ languages |
| Auth | Lucia Auth v3 | Passwordless (magic links + SMS OTP) |
| Sunset Calculation | suncalc (client-side) | No API dependency, works offline |

### 9.2 Why This Stack?

- **Cost**: Infrastructure costs < $0.02 per church per month at scale (see [cost model](TECHNICAL_ARCHITECTURE.md#12-cost-model-infrastructure)) — sustainable at $7/church/month.
- **Offline-first**: PWA + IndexedDB + CRDT sync means volunteers work without internet and data syncs when a connection returns.
- **Global**: Cloudflare's edge network spans 330+ cities; churches in Fiji, Zambia, Brazil, or India all get sub-100ms latency.
- **No app store**: Churches install from a URL; no Apple/Google approval delays; automatic updates via service worker.

## 10. User Onboarding & Migration

### 10.1 New Church Setup

1. **Registration** (2 minutes): Church clerk visits the app URL → enters church name, address (auto-geocoded for timezone and sunset calculation), selects preferred language → receives admin magic link via email or SMS.
2. **Subscription** (1 minute): Enters card details or selects annual invoice. A **14-day free trial** begins immediately with full access to all 19 modules. No credit card required to start the trial.
3. **First Data Entry** (3 options):
   - **Invite by link**: Share a signup URL in the church WhatsApp or Facebook group; members self-register with name, phone, and email.
   - **CSV upload**: Download a template → fill in member names and contacts → upload. The system maps columns and imports.
   - **Manual entry**: Add members one at a time.
4. **Quick Setup Wizard** (5 minutes): Guided walkthrough — "Who are your volunteers?" (assign roles), "Set up your first Sabbath service rota", "Try a test donation" → lands on the role-appropriate dashboard.

| Existing System | Migration Method |
|-----------------|------------------|
| Excel / Google Sheets | CSV upload with column-mapping UI |
| Paper records | Bulk photo upload → OCR processing → treasurer review queue |
| ACMS / eAdventist | API import adapter (Phase 3 roadmap) |

A **Migration Assistant** mode lets volunteers work through a queue of imported records, confirming or correcting each one.

### 10.3 Member Invitation

The church admin generates an **invitation link** — shared in the church communication channel. Members tap the link, enter their name and phone number, and their profile is created. The admin reviews and approves new profiles (or auto-approves if configured).

## 11. Roles & Permissions

Theobase uses a **Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)** model. Every volunteer account is assigned exactly one role per church. Roles gate every endpoint, query, and UI element.

| Role | Assigned To | Core Access |
|------|------------|-------------|
| **Church Admin** | Clerk | Full CRUD on all church data; manage subscriptions and roles; view audit logs |
| **Pastor** | Pastor, District Pastor | View all church data; edit pastoral records, board minutes, district hub |
| **Treasurer** | Church Treasurer, Assistant Treasurer | View and approve finances; manage budgets; export reports; **cannot** edit member profiles or board minutes |
| **Board Chair** | Head Elder | View all; chair board meetings (run votes); manage duty rota |
| **Department Leader** | Youth, Pathfinder, Sabbath School, Welfare, AV, Dorcas leaders | Full CRUD on their assigned department module; view-only on member directory |
| **Volunteer** | Deacon, Deaconess, Teacher, Counselor | View assigned duties; mark attendance for assigned classes; submit welfare notes |
| **Member** | All church members | View own profile and giving history; see duty schedule; upload donation receipts; update contact info |

**Three modules use elevated confidentiality** beyond standard RBAC: the Nominating Vault (anonymous ballots, encrypted at rest), the Welfare CRM (recipient names encrypted), and the Safety Shield (background check documents encrypted per-church).

## 14. Legal & Compliance

### 14.1 Theobase Is Not a Financial Institution

Theobase does not hold, transmit, process, route, or touch donation money. It functions as a **digital filing cabinet and verification ledger**. Members transfer funds directly from their personal bank or mobile money accounts into their church's official bank account. Theobase stores only the **screenshot receipt** and **allocation breakdown**. This design means Theobase:

- Requires no banking license in any jurisdiction.
- Incurs no payment processing fees, interchange fees, or percentage cuts.
- Has no PCI-DSS compliance burden.
- Bears no liability for fund transfers, reversals, or disputes.

### 14.2 Data Protection

| Regulation | Compliance Measure |
|------------|-------------------|
| **GDPR** (Europe) | Data stored in EU Cloudflare region; member data export (JSON); right to erasure (delete encrypted PII + destroy key); consent tracking; Data Processing Agreement available |
| **CCPA/CPRA** (California) | Member data export; deletion on request; opt-out of data sale (though no data is sold) |
| **PIPL** (China) | Data stored within China-accessible regions; separate deployment if needed |
| **Pacific Islands** (no comprehensive laws) | GDPR-equivalent protections applied as the global standard |

### 14.3 Child Data Protection

The platform tracks children in Pathfinders (#9), Adventurers (#9), and Sabbath School (#10). Compliance measures:

- **Parental consent**: Required before any child profile is created. Consent is recorded with timestamp and IP.
- **Data minimization**: Only name, age, class progress, and parent contact are stored. No biometrics, no location tracking, no behavioral data.
- **Parental access**: Parents can view and export all data stored about their children at any time.
- **Deletion**: Parents can request deletion of their child's data at any time.

### 14.4 Key Documents

The following legal documents will be published before public launch:

- **Terms of Service** — church-level agreement covering subscription, acceptable use, liability limitations
- **Privacy Policy** — plain-language explanation of what data is stored, where, and why
- **Data Processing Agreement (DPA)** — for churches in GDPR jurisdictions
- **Subprocessors List** — Cloudflare (hosting), Resend (email), Twilio (SMS)
- **Security Whitepaper** — technical overview of encryption, access controls, and audit logging

### 14.5 Data Breach Response

In the event of unauthorized access to member data:
1. **Containment** within 24 hours (revoke keys, isolate affected resources)
2. **Notification** to affected church admins within 72 hours
3. **Public disclosure** if required by law
4. **Post-incident report** published within 30 days

### 14.6 Denominational Relationship

Theobase is an independent technology provider. While designed specifically for Seventh-day Adventist church operations, it is not owned, operated, or endorsed by the General Conference of Seventh-day Adventists or any union, conference, or mission. The platform is designed to **complement** existing denominational systems, not replace them. Churches retain full ownership of their data at all times.

## 18. Product Analytics (Privacy-Safe)

All analytics must respect that church member data is sensitive. The analytics strategy uses only **anonymized, aggregate, role-scoped telemetry**:

| Metric | Method | Privacy Safeguard |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| Feature adoption (which modules are active) | Church-level flag: "Module X has ≥ 1 action this month" | No member-level data; church identity hashed |
| User activity (DAU/WAU/MAU per church) | Counter incremented per role per church | No individual user IDs; only count of active roles |
| Funnel analysis (signup → first member → first receipt → first rota) | Church-level stage tracking | Aggregated across cohort; no individual church identifiable in < 10-church cohorts |
| Error tracking | Sentry (server-side), anonymized client crash reports | No PII in error context; source IP truncated |
| Performance (LCP, FID, CLS) | Cloudflare Web Analytics | No cookies; no fingerprinting |

**What is NOT tracked**: Individual member behavior, donation amounts, attendance patterns, content of board minutes, welfare cases, or any data that could identify a specific church member.

## 20. Risk Register

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Low smartphone adoption in target regions | Medium | High | SMS-based interaction for members without smartphones; treasurer desktop access |
| Expensive mobile data deters usage | High | Medium | PWA < 5 MB; compressed sync payloads; WiFi-preferred uploads; offline-first means infrequent sync |
| Church resistance to digital tools | Medium | High | Free nominating committee tool as warm introduction; 14-day free trial; no credit card to start; migration assistance |
| OCR accuracy insufficient for Pacific island names | Medium | Medium | Confidence score display with easy correction; manual entry fallback; train custom Tesseract data over time |
| Browser IndexedDB eviction | Low | High | Persistent storage API; automatic cloud backup on every sync; warn user on low storage |
| DDoS or abuse targeting churches | Low | Medium | Cloudflare DDoS protection; per-IP rate limiting; audit logging for abuse detection |
| Denominational conflict with official systems | Low | Medium | Position as complementary, not replacement; data export to official formats; engage conferences proactively |
| Single point of failure (small team) | Medium | High | Fully documented architecture; automated CI/CD; managed cloud services minimize operational burden; build open-source community |
| Regulatory change in data residency laws | Low | Medium | Regional data placement from day one; per-region DPA templates; legal counsel retainer |
| Currency fluctuation for $7 USD pricing | Medium | Low | Annual prepaid option locks in rate; regional pricing adjustments possible |
