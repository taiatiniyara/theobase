# THEOBASE — Distribution Engine

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Draft from grilling session  
**Cost:** $0 — built into the platform. No paid acquisition, no ads, no sales team.

---

## Core Insight

Theobase spreads through **officer-to-officer contact at in-person denominational events** — camp meetings, training days, conference sessions. The officer who uses Theobase daily is the most credible carrier. They have hands-on experience, they have saved real time, and they naturally talk to peers from other churches at the same gatherings.

The distribution engine does not create new behavior. It **amplifies and rewards behavior that already happens** — an officer showing a peer something that works.

---

## The Four Layers

### Layer 1: The Clean-Report Halo

Every quarter, the conference sends a reporting-status summary to all churches. Theobase churches appear **green** — complete, on-time, verified. Manual churches appear **amber** (late, incomplete) or **red** (not submitted).

No one has to say anything. The gap is visible.

This is passive, recurring, and scales to every conference using Theobase. A non-Theobase treasurer whose church shows up red for the third consecutive quarter feels the pressure without anyone naming it. A Theobase clerk whose church is consistently green gains quiet credibility.

The halo is the largest-scale mechanic — it reaches every church in every subscribing conference, whether or not any individual officer is an active promoter.

---

### Layer 2: Demo Mode

When an officer from Church A meets an officer from Church B at a camp meeting or training event, the demo flow works like this:

1. Officer A opens Theobase and taps **"Show a Friend."**
2. The app enters a **guided demo mode** — data is blurred, but the workflows are live: log a baptism, count an offering, record a board motion, generate a quarterly report.
3. Officer A hands the phone to Officer B. Officer B walks through the demo hands-on.
4. At the end, the app offers: **"Send yourself a setup link?"** Officer B enters their phone number. The link arrives via SMS or WhatsApp.
5. If Officer B signs up within 90 days, the referral is credited to Officer A.

Demo mode is the conversion engine. It converts a conversation ("you should try this") into an experience ("I just tried it — this is faster than what I do now").

---

### Layer 3: Badges and the Impact Tab

Every officer has a private **Impact** tab in their Theobase app. It is visible only to them. It shows:

**Usage badges** (automated — earned naturally by using the platform):
- First baptism logged
- First offering counted
- First board motion recorded
- First quarterly report submitted
- First annual statistical report completed

**Consistency streaks** (automated — earned by sustained use):
- 3 consecutive months of on-time reporting
- 6 months active
- 12 months active (1 year)
- 1 year without a late remittance

**Referral badges** (automated — earned by spreading adoption):
- First church referred
- 3 churches in pilot
- 5 churches in pilot
- 10 churches referred
- District fully onboarded (all churches in the district using Theobase)

**Leadership endorsements** (manual — granted by conferences/unions):
- Conference Recommended Church
- Union Featured Church
- Pilot Champion

Usage badges reward onboarding effort. Streak badges reward retention. Referral badges reward distribution. Leadership badges give conferences a lever to spotlight exemplars — which in turn become demo-mode hosts at the next camp meeting.

---

### Layer 4: The Conference Nudge

The revenue model depends on conferences deciding to pay for their churches. This layer closes that gap.

30 days before a church's 6-month pilot expires, the officer sees a prompt:
> *"Your pilot ends in 30 days. Would you like us to ask your conference to cover your church?"*

One tap. The conference treasurer receives an email:
> *"12 of your churches have asked to continue using Theobase. Monthly cost: $36. Annual cost: $432. Reply YES to approve coverage for all 12."*

The conference treasurer says yes — or doesn't. The nudge surfaces the decision at the moment it's relevant, with a specific count and cost, requiring only a reply. Combined with the quarterly report (which shows the reporting-quality gap), the conference has both the data and the trigger.

## What the Engine Does Not Do

| Avoided mechanic | Why |
|---|---|
| Public leaderboard of officers | Feels like a corporate sales dashboard. Shame when a church falls off. Undermines trust. The Impact tab is private. |
| Referral bonuses or monetary rewards | Erodes authenticity. Officers should refer because the tool saves time, not because they get paid. Changes the nature of the peer relationship. |
| In-app notifications to non-users | No spam. The engine spreads through real relationships at real events, not through push notifications to people who never opted in. |
| Email drip campaigns or marketing automation | No outbound marketing. Theobase grows through the product itself and through the SDA church's existing meeting rhythms. |
| Paid ads or sponsored posts | Against the zero-cost principle and the denominational-positioning principle. This is church infrastructure, not a startup. |

## Growth Flywheel

```
  Officer uses Theobase daily
           │
           ▼
  Officer's church shows green on quarterly report
           │
           ▼
  Officer shows peer at camp meeting (demo mode)
           │
           ▼
  Peer signs up (referral credited)
           │
           ▼
  Peer's church enters 6-month pilot
           │
           ▼
  Peer's church shows green on next quarterly report
           │
           ▼
  Conference sees growing adoption + clean data
           │
           ▼
  Conference receives nudge at pilot expiry → commits
           │
           ▼
  Conference pays. More churches onboarded.
           │
           ▼
  (loop repeats with newly onboarded officers as carriers)
```

The flywheel runs on the SDA church's natural meeting calendar. Camp meetings happen annually. Officer training days happen quarterly or annually. Every cycle is a new distribution event — no marketing calendar needed.
